Digital locks are becoming a common upgrade on Wellington homes, especially where owners want easier access control, cleaner entry, and fewer key callouts.

If you are comparing systems, three we regularly get asked about are the **Elock system**, **Yale YDM system**, and **Doric DE10**.

## Why digital locks are gaining traction
- No spare keys under mats or in letterboxes.
- Faster tenant or family access changes.
- Better day-to-day convenience on high-use entry doors.
- Useful for owners managing multiple properties.

## Elock, Yale YDM, and Doric DE10: quick overview
### Elock system
Commonly chosen for simple keypad access and straightforward day-to-day use. Good option where you want a practical digital entry setup without overcomplicating the door hardware.

### Yale YDM system
Well-known digital lock range with multiple access modes depending on model (PIN / card / app / key override). Popular with owners wanting broader feature options.

### Doric DE10
A strong fit for Australian/NZ style door hardware ecosystems where compatibility and proven local support matter. Often selected when a clean, reliable retrofit is the priority.

## What matters more than the brand
The lock is only one part of the job. On aluminium joinery, long-term reliability depends on:
- Correct door alignment and latch engagement.
- Proper prep of existing hardware cut-outs.
- Door thickness/backset compatibility.
- Weather exposure and sealing details.
- Safe handover so users can operate and manage codes correctly.

If the door is already misaligned or the latch is binding, a digital lock can feel unreliable even when the lock itself is good.

## Can digital locks be retrofitted to aluminium doors?
Usually yes, but it depends on existing hardware and profile constraints. We assess what is already installed, then recommend the cleanest approach for fitment and ongoing serviceability.
